Object of the Game

Each player assumes the role of either a Human or Alien. The Humans are trying to save themselves by reaching the Escape Pods. The Aliens seek to hunt down all the Humans.

Each player's identity and position is kept secret; You will need to interpret their movement and behaviour to discover who and what they really are!

  • Each Human player acts alone, trying to be the first to reach one of the Escape Pods
  • The Aliens must work together to intercept and kill the Humans before they reach an Escape Pod

At the beginning of the game you don't know what side the other players are on! You will need to deduce their identity by watching their actions. Your team can be seen by hovering over the card in the top-righthand corner of your screen.

The Map

Each game will take place on one of the maps available here and will look something like this:

A grid of hexagons has been placed to control movement. Each hexagon is called a Sector and every Sector is identified by a unique letter & number combination

Sectors are colored differently based on what type of Sector they are, and each type will be explained in the below.

Game Turns

The game is played over a series of rounds. During each round, each player must move his or her character. Your character type determines the distance they can move:

  • Humans move only ONE sector
  • Aliens move ONE or TWO sectors

Your location is marked by a Gold sector. On your turn, your available moves will be outlined in Gold as well. Unless otherwise noted, you can never end your turn in the same sector your started in. YOU MUST MOVE EVERY ROUND

Types of Sectors

There are 5 different types of Sectors. After moving, the type of Sector you moved into determines what happens next:

Safe Sector

Safe Sectors are marked in White. If you move into a Safe Sector, you will make no noise.

Dangerous Sector

Dangerous Sectors are marked in Gray. If you move into a Dangerous Sector, you will draw a card. Depending on what card is drawn, you will make noise accordingly. This is explained more below.

Human Sector

Human Sectors are marked in Blue. This is where Human players start - if there are multiple Human Sectors, Humans will be randomly placed in one of them at the beginning of the game. Players cannot move into or through these Sectors.

Alien Sector

Alien Sectors are marked in Red. This is where Alien players start - if there are multiple Alien Sectors, Aliens will be randomly placed in one of them at the beginning of the game. Players cannot move into or through these Sectors.

Escape Pod Sectors

Escape Pods are marked in Green. Humans must end their turn in one of these Sectors to win. Only Humans can move into these Sectors. These are explained more below.

Dangerous Sectors

If you move into a Dangerous Sector, you will draw a Card. There are 4 main types of cards you might draw:

  • Red "Noise In Your Sector" Cards

    If you draw a Red Card, you will make noise in the Sector you just moved into.

  • Green "Noise In Any Sector" Cards

    If you draw a Green Card, you may pick any Sector on the map to make noise in.

  • White "Silence" Cards

    If you draw a White Card, you will avoid making any noise on that turn.

  • White "Item" Cards

    If you draw an Item card, you will avoid making any noise that turn. Additionally, if you're a Human, you will add that Item to your hand, which is shown at the bottom of the screen. Cards can be used at any point on your turn after you've drawn them, including on the turn you draw the Item. Alien Players cannot receive Item cards and will discard them. For an explanation of what each Item card does, you can hover over that card in your hand, or check out the Card page in the Compendium.

In any game, before it starts, the Host can adjust how many of each card is active in the game.

Attacking

Alien Players may decide to attack at the end of his or her turn. After moving, you will be asked if you'd like to attack the Sector you just moved into. If you select "No", your turn will continue as normal. If you select "Yes", the following will happen:

  1. You will not draw any cards this turn, regardless of what type of Sector you just moved into.
  2. It will be announced that you are attacking the Sector you just moved into.
  3. Any other players in that Sector are immediately killed, regardless of whether they are Human or Alien.
  4. Humans that are killed will respawn on their next turn as an Alien in an Alien Start Sector. Killed Aliens are (by default) permanently killed and will become a Spectator. The Host has an option to change this, instead letting killed Aliens respawn as well.
  5. If an Alien kills a Human, that Alien will be given the Hyperphagic Status Effect. This will allow you to move 1 extra space every turn for the rest of the game. This bonus does not increase if an Alien kills multiple Humans.

Character Roles

Character Roles give each player unique abilities for you to use in the game. Players do not have to reveal their Role at any point, so feel free to keep it a secret if it benefits you! In any game, before it starts, the Host has control over which roles are active, how many of each role can be in the game, and whether any roles should be guaranteed to be in the game.

For an explanation of each role, see that page in the Compendium

Escape Pods

When a Human player ends their turn in one of the Escape Pods, the following will happen:

  1. That player will draw one of the Escape Pod cards to see if it's functional. By default, there are 4 "Working Pod" cards and 1 "Broken Pod" card. The number of working vs. broken Escape Pod cards can be set by the Host before the game starts.
  2. If the pod is working, that player has successfully escaped! They will be removed from the board and the player will be placed on the Spectator team
  3. If the pod is broken, the pod is damaged and doesn't work. That player will need to find a different Escape Pod.
  4. Regardless of whether the pod is working or broken, it can no longer be used by anyone in the game. It will be marked in Brown to signify it can no longer be used.

The End of the Game

The game ends after 40 turns (by default - the Host can adjust this value) or when there are no Human players remaining.

  • Any Human that successfully escaped wins!
  • The Aliens win if they kill the last Human on board.
  • If the set number of turns have elapsed, the ship runs out of air. Any remaining Humans suffocate and the Aliens win!

That should be everything! Above all, Remember: This is a game of bluff and guile. You are very much encouraged to chat with the other players during the game, discussing strategies, making accusations, and ideally pretending to be part of the opposing team!

The Overlay

A number of elements have been positioned around the edge of your screen during play for information. Any overlay (unless otherwise noted) can be expanded by simply hovering your cursor over it.

  1. Event Log

    Hover over this to see a list of all players in the game. Clicking on a player will bring up a recap of every Game Alert they've been mentioned in, including noises, attacks, deaths, and card plays.

  2. Team Card

    Hover over this to see the team you're on.

  3. Role Card

    Hover over this to see the Role you've been assigned. A tooltip will also appear detailing exactly what that Role does

  4. Status Effects

    Hover over this to see what Status Effects are currently affecting you, as well as how many stacks you have. You can hover over each Status Effect individually to see what that Status Effect does.

  5. Your Hand (Humans Only)

    Hover over this to see what cards are currently in your hand. Each card will have a tooltip upon hovering over it detailing what that card does. You can click on a card at any point during your turn to play it immediately.

  6. Turn Order

    Hover over this to see the list of all players in the game, as well as who's turn it is (marked in green) and the order of play.

  7. Drawing Menu

    Click this to activate Drawing Mode. While in drawing mode, you can freely draw on the map with one of the preselected colors or one of your own choice. There is also a brush width selector, eraser, and an "Erase All" button (displayed as a Trash Can in the bottom-right)
