Status Effects

Status Effects are a mechanic I've created for this version of the game for two reasons: First, for ease of implementation for Cards & Roles that don't have immediate effects, and Second, for more possibilities for interesting new mechanics I can add to the game. A Status Effect is given to a player by certain Items and Roles, and their effect will automatically activate when their criteria is met. See below for details about each one!

French Vanilla

These Status Effects are effects given by Vanilla Cards & Roles.

Adrenaline Surge

Received by playing an Adrenaline card or being assigned the Fast Alien role. Allows you to move 1 space farther the next time you move. Currently, a stack will be consumed regardless of whether you take advantage of the extra space of movement.

Armored

Received by playing a Defense card or being assigned the Brute Alien role. If you are attacked while having this Status Effect, the Attack has no effect against you.

Cloned

Received by playing a Clone card. If you die while having this Status Effect, you will immediately respawn in a randomly selected Human Start sector. You will remain on the Human Team.

Deceptive

Received by being assigned the Psychic Alien role. Upon drawing a White Card (either Item cards or the White Noise card), instead of making no noise, you may make noise in any sector as if you had drawn a Green Card.

Feline

Received by playing a Cat card. The next time you make a noise, you may choose an extra sector to make noise in. For example, if you draw a Red Card and have this Status Effect, everyone else will see you make 2 noises - one where you are and one of your choosing. If you draw a Green Card and have this Status Effect, you can choose 2 sectors to make noise in. Drawing White (Item or White Noise) cards will not trigger this effect.

Hyperphagic

Received (only for Aliens) by killing a Human. You may permanently move 1 extra space on every turn.

Invisible

Received by being assigned the Invisible Alien role. You are immune to Spotlight and Sensor cards, and will not have your position revealed by those cards.

Knowhow

Received by being assigned the Engineer Human role. You draw 2 Escape Pod cards when you reach an escape pod.

Lurking

Received by being assigned the Lurking Alien or Executive Officer Human roles. You are allowed to end your turn in the same sector you started in

Sedated

Received by playing a Sedatives card or being assigned the Captain Human role. You will treat the next sector you move into as a Safe Sector, regardless of whether it's a Dangerous Sector or Safe Sector
